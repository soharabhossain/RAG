Sherlock Holmes is a fictional detective created by British author Sir Arthur Conan Doyle. He is one of the most famous and enduring fictional detectives in literature. The character of Sherlock Holmes made his first appearance in the novel "A Study in Scarlet," which was published in 1887.

Sherlock Holmes is known for his brilliant deductive reasoning, keen observation skills, and logical approach to solving complex mysteries and crimes. He lived at 221B Baker Street, London, and was accompanied by his loyal friend and biographer, Dr. John H. Watson, who chronicled his adventures.

Throughout the series of four novels and fifty-six short stories written by Arthur Conan Doyle, Sherlock Holmes became an iconic figure in detective fiction. Some of the most popular and well-known stories include "The Hound of the Baskervilles," "The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes," "The Memoirs of Sherlock Holmes," and "The Return of Sherlock Holmes."

The character of Sherlock Holmes has been adapted into numerous films, television shows, radio dramas, and stage productions over the years. His enduring popularity has made him a cultural icon and a symbol of intelligence and astute reasoning in detective work.
